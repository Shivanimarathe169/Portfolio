Step 8: Add Attachments or Format Text

The toolbar at the bottom of the email editor provides several tools to enhance your email. These include text formatting options, the ability to attach files or images, and more. Proper use of these features ensures a professional and visually appealing email.

Steps to Add Attachments to Email:

  1. Open Gmail and click on "Compose" to start a new email.

  2. Click the paperclip icon at the bottom of the email compose window on the toolbar.

  3. Browse and select the file you want to attach, then click "Open" or "Choose" to upload.

  4. Wait for the attachment to upload fully (you'll see the file name appear once it is uploaded).

  5. Review your attachment and ensure it’s the correct file.

  6. Complete your email and click "Send."

Use the same toolbar to apply various features:

  1. A Icon: Format text (bold, italics, underline).

  2. Paperclip Icon: Attach files to the email.

  3. Link Icon: Add cloud links (e.g., Google Drive links) to your emails.

  4. Smiley Face Icon: Add emojis to your emails.

  5. Drive Icon: Attach files directly from Google Drive (cloud storage).

  6. Image Icon: Attach images to your email.

  7. Lock Icon: Turn on confidential mode to prevent recipients from forwarding, copying, printing, or downloading the email.

  8. Pen Icon: Add a digital signature manually or set up an automatic signature in email settings.

  9. Three Dots Menu: Access additional options like discarding, saving as a draft, or printing the email.
